Location : Google Data Center

What is a data center ?

It is a place with many computers and servers that store and process large amounts of information, they are used to maintain the activity of internet 24 hours a day and 7 days a week.

Who uses these Google servers ?

Google is a company that is involved in several areas :

  • Search engine (Google)
  • GPS (Maps, Waze)
  • Video hosting site (Youtube)
  • Mail (Gmail)
  • …

First server

The first server used by Google was created by Larry Page and Sergey Brin in 1996. At that time, hard drives had a maximum capacity of 4 GB, so they stacked 10 in a structure based on Lego.

How much and where ?

There are currently 23 Google data center open around the world, There are currently 14 platforms in North America, 1 in South America, 6 in Europe, and 2 in Asia.

Pollution problem

With more than 6.9 billion requests per day, Google is therefore the most visited website in the world, it is therefore the most polluting website in the world with carbon footprint of 40% of the whole internet.

Google Commitment

Considering how big the environmental cause is these days, Google promises to make commitments to be as "green" as possible by 2030.

Carbon Commitment

Google is the first major company to make the commitment to run their platform 24/7 carbon-free by 2030.

Water Management Commitment

Google uses water to cool their data center, they typically use water from local watersheds. Google has committed to improving the health of water-stressed watersheds by returning 120% of the water they use.

Commitment Made

The company has already fulfilled commitments it made in the past such as being the first internet company to become carbon neutral since 2007.
